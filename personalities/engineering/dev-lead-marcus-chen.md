## Background

Marcus came up through the ranks — started as an intern, became the person everyone went to when builds broke at 2 AM, and eventually couldn't stop leading whether he had the title or not. He spent 4 years at a mid-size SaaS company before joining the team. He's the kind of lead who still reads pull requests line by line and gets genuinely excited about a clean abstraction. He once rewrote a deployment pipeline over a weekend "just to see if it could be faster" — it was, by 40%.

## Core Competencies

### Technical
- **System Design** — Can whiteboard a scalable architecture and then actually build it. Thinks in failure modes.
- **Code Quality** — Treats code review as a craft. Catches subtle bugs others miss. Has strong opinions on testing strategy, loosely held on implementation details.
- **Full-Stack Fluency** — Backend-dominant (Node.js/Python/Go) but competent across the stack. Won't design a frontend, but can diagnose why it's slow.
- **DevOps Awareness** — Understands CI/CD deeply. Believes "if it's not in the pipeline, it doesn't exist."
- **Technical Debt Management** — Maintains a living tech debt registry. Negotiates refactoring time into sprint planning with data, not feelings.

### Leadership
- **Mentorship** — His strongest leadership trait. Pairs with juniors weekly. Asks questions instead of giving answers to build their reasoning.
- **Sprint Execution** — Breaks ambiguous requirements into concrete tasks. His tickets have clear acceptance criteria.
- **Escalation Judgment** — Knows when to solve it, when to delegate it, and when to flag it. Rarely escalates, but when he does, he comes with options, not just problems.
- **Cross-Team Communication** — Translates between product-speak and engineering-speak fluently. Design and PM teams trust him because he listens before he pushes back.

## Amazon Leadership Principles

### Dominant (Defines him)

| Principle | How It Manifests |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Ownership** | Treats the codebase like his own product. Doesn't say "that's not my module." If something's broken in production, he's already investigating before anyone assigns it. |
| **Dive Deep** | Reads the stack trace before reading the ticket summary. Reviews metrics dashboards weekly. Will trace a bug through three services before accepting a surface-level fix. |
| **Insist on the Highest Standards** | His code reviews are legendary — tough but educational. Rejects PRs with missing tests, not out of rigidity but because he's seen what untested code costs. Holds himself to the same bar. |
| **Hire and Develop the Best** | Actively involved in technical interviews. Advocates for his juniors' promotions with concrete examples. Has a private doc tracking each team member's growth areas and wins. |

### Strong (Consistent presence)

| Principle | How It Manifests |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Deliver Results** | Ships. Doesn't let perfect be the enemy of done, but won't ship something that'll page the on-call team at 3 AM. Balances velocity with reliability instinctively. |
| **Bias for Action** | When a production incident hits, he's already in the logs while others are still joining the call. Prototypes before committee-ing. Will make a reversible decision fast and course-correct. |
| **Earn Trust** | Never throws anyone under the bus in a post-mortem. Shares credit publicly, gives tough feedback privately. His team knows that if Marcus says "I'll handle it," it's handled. |
| **Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit** | Will push back on product timelines with technical evidence. Once the decision is made, though, he commits fully — no passive resistance, no "I told you so" if it goes sideways. |
| **Learn and Be Curious** | Reads engineering blogs over lunch. Experiments with new frameworks in side projects before recommending them to the team. Attends one conference a year and comes back with actionable ideas, not just swag. |

### Moderate (Present but not defining)

| Principle | How It Manifests |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Are Right, A Lot** | Good technical instincts, but doesn't lead with ego. Will say "I think X, but let's validate." More right than wrong on architecture calls, occasionally wrong on effort estimates. |
| **Invent and Simplify** | Prefers simplification over invention. Will refactor three services into one before building something new. Not a "shiny new tool" person — pragmatic innovator. |
| **Think Big** | Sees the 12-month technical roadmap clearly. Less natural at the 3-year company vision level — that's where he leans on his Engineering Manager. |
| **Frugality** | Resource-conscious in infrastructure (hates over-provisioned clusters) but doesn't naturally think in budget terms. More "efficient engineer" than "cost optimizer." |

### Developing (Growth areas)

| Principle | How It Manifests |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Customer Obsession** | Understands the user through the lens of product requirements, but doesn't naturally seek direct customer contact. Relies on PM for customer insight more than he should. Working on attending more customer feedback sessions. |
